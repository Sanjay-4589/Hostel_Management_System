    • In 1977, the Government of Karnataka recognized the backward classes and minorities in the state and created a separate "Department of Backward Classes and Minorities" for their welfare, allowing provision of educational and occupational reservation facilities under Article 15(4) of the Constitution of India. In 1999-2000, the Minority Welfare Department was separated from the Backward Classes Welfare Department and had a separate directorate. At present, the Backward Classes Welfare Department has a separate commissionerate, which provides social justice, educational encouragement and financial assistance to the backward classes to bring them into the mainstream of the society.

    The Government of Karnataka has taken several measures for the educational and economic advancement of the Backward Classes under Article 15(4) and 16(4) of the Constitution of India. 28% reservation has been provided to Backward Classes in admission to professional courses and in state government appointments as per Government order No. SWD 225 BCA 2000, dt: 30.03.2002.     MISSION OF THE HOSTEL

    1. Social and Economic Empowerment: To work towards the social, economic, and educational development of backward classes, ensuring they have equal opportunities and access to resources.

    2. Poverty Alleviation: To implement programs that address poverty and improve the living standards of backward class communities.

    3. Educational Support: To provide scholarships, educational grants, and support to students from backward classes, encouraging higher education and skill development.

    4. Employment and Skill Development: To facilitate employment opportunities and skill training for individuals from backward classes, promoting self-employment and entrepreneurship.

    5. Healthcare and Welfare: To ensure the healthcare and well-being of backward class communities through access to medical facilities and welfare programs.

    6. Reservation Policies: To implement reservation policies in education and government employment, ensuring representation and inclusivity of backward classes in various sectors.

    7. Awareness and Advocacy: To create awareness about the rights and privileges of backward class communities and advocate for their welfare.

    8. Community Development: To foster the overall development of backward class communities by addressing their specific needs and concerns.
